Revisions

Every time you save a post or page, Pubvana automatically creates a revision snapshot so you can roll back to any earlier version.

How Revisions Work

On each save, the current state of the post (title, content, excerpt, status) is captured as a revision. The last 20 revisions are kept per post; older ones are automatically pruned.

Viewing Revision History

Open a post for editing and scroll to the Revisions panel at the bottom. Each revision shows:

  • Date and time of the save
  • Author who made the change
  • A preview of the content at that point

Restoring a Previous Version

Click Restore next to the revision you want. The editor loads that version’s content. Review it, then click Save Post to make it the active version. The restore itself is recorded as a new revision.

Tip Restoring a revision does not immediately publish it — it just populates the editor. You can still make further edits before saving.
